Synopsis

"I Have a Dream" - speech by Martin Luther King

On August 28th 1963, a human rights activist, Martin Luther King Jr. delivered a speech during the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom. The speech came to be one of the most popular and iconic speeches in recorded history known as “I have a dream”. The speech was heard by approximately two hundred and fifty thousand civil-right supporters and citizens alike who were at the time, near the steps of Lincoln Memorial. King was one of the eighteen people to speak on that day. He spoke out about how in America, African-American people were oppressed even though the Emancipation Proclamation was signed by Lincoln a century earlier.

MLK Jr. begins his speech by expressing his joy to be one and together with the crowd that has come to demonstrate their willingness for their freedom as well as others. King emphasised on “All Men are Created Equal” stating that no matter what you are, which gender you are, the colour of the skin, or which religion you believe in, as long as you are a human, we all are entitled to the same rights and opportunities as anyone else. He spoke up for the Black people living in the States, his people, who were enduring immense oppression, discrimination, and segregation for centuries. A hundred years ago the Negros were freed from slavery, but King stated in that very speech and I quote “One hundred years later, the Negro still is not free.” – implying that they are just not slaves anymore but there is nothing for them except that, and not getting the full spectrum of freedom they deserve as a human being living in a society. He also included in his speech, the world he dreamt of where racism, segregation, and discrimination of any kind does not exist. King then adds “Let freedom ring from” – and inserts geographical locations such as states, cities and towns to get more relatability from the audience to his speech, inspiring them.

To conclude, Martin is a admirable man with a great vision and with ambitious dreams for the future of society. He made it known that if his visions will be a reality someday if not in his generation. He repeated the words “I have a dream” many times describing a scenario where people with colour lives their lives freely without any segregation or discrimination. Due to his efforts and many other like him, we now live in a world where there are fewer racist people and discrimination in the air. The world today is not perfect. But we all need to contribute to achieve a world which is united as one.

Dr. Martin Luther King

Dr. Martin Luther King

Martin Luther King was an American Civil-Rights activist and a Baptist Minister, born on the 15th January 1929 in the State Atlanta, United States. He got involved in labour rights and desegregation of black people marked a critical turning point in the South's and other regions of the country's efforts to repeal laws that discriminated against African Americans. He is well-known for his famous speech I Have a Dream, delivered in 1963. With this speech, King was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize at the age of 35, for his non-violent opposition and demonstration.


On the 4th April 1964, While planning a national occupation of Washington, D.C. in Memphis, Tennessee. His death sparked riots in a number of American cities. Nonetheless, he was honored with the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1977 and the Congressional Gold Medal in 2003 for his achievements.

There are many similarities between the speech "I have a dream" and the novel Brave New World as there are differences. In the story if the Nrave New World is set in a dystopian future where all humans are grown in labs, genetically engineered castes that has their own purposes limiting the intelligence level of a person, conditioning them on what they are supposed to like or hate. The castes are differentiated by the colour of clothes they wear and their heights. They are segreated by where they live and what type of works they do. But because of the advanced technology of the World State, and the drug they use (SOMA) keeps them happy and makes it less likely for them to me rebellious like the real world.


One thing that contridicts in the speech and the novel is the theme of the speech – "All Men are created Equal". In the Brave New World, all men aren't created equal. Humans are lab-grown with predetermined intelligence level and physical chatacteristics according to the predetermined caste. In the World State, because of this humans are born segregated, and differentiated like livestock catagorized into groups with the most meat. The sad thing about this is that most of the citizens of the World State does not know about this. They are busy taking soma and supporting consumerism which are highly encouraged in the world of the Brave New World.In the real world where people like King exists to change the ways that we or some people have been living and change the customs and rules that was previously used to segregate and discriminate people.


The Book's author emphasises on how the citizens of the World State are comfortable and satisfied with the way they are in the story and never wanting to get out of their comfort zone and find out how their world works. This is because the author of Brave New World, Aldous Huxley, wanted to warn us about an all powerful government with advanced technology, controlling every aspect of out lifes. But King's mentality is that being operessed is very bad and you must do anything to get free of it. Even if it needs to be getting out of your confort zone, it is worth it.
